Injera

Injera is a traditional Ethiopian flatbread that is made from teff flour and is typically served with a variety of stews and dishes.Injera is a nutritious food that is high in protein, fiber, and other important nutrients. It is also gluten-free, which makes it a popular choice for people with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease.

prep time

5 min.

cook time

20 min.

serving

4 servings

Ingredients

2 cups teff flour

3 cups water

2 cups water

1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast

Salt to taste

Instructions

step 1

In a large mixing bowl, combine the teff flour and water, whisking well to create a smooth batter. If using yeast, sprinkle it over the batter and stir it in.

step 2

Cover the bowl with a clean towel and let it sit at room temperature for 24-48 hours. This fermentation process gives the injera its distinct sourdough flavor and spongy texture.

step 3

After 24-48 hours, the batter will be bubbly and have a slightly sour smell. Stir the batter well and add salt to taste.

step 4

Preheat a non-stick skillet or injera pan over medium heat.

step 5

Use a ladle to pour about 1/4 to 1/2 cup of the batter onto the pan, spreading it evenly into a thin circle. The injera should be about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick.

step 6

Cover the pan with a lid and let the injera cook for 1-2 minutes, or until the surface is covered in small holes and the edges begin to lift from the pan.

step 7

Remove the injera from the pan and let it cool on a wire rack. Repeat the process with the remaining batter until all the injera is cooked.
