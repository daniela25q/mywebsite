Ceviche

Ceviche is a popular seafood dish that is commonly found in Latin American cuisine, particularly in coastal regions. It typically consists of raw fish or seafood that is marinated in citrus juices, such as lime or lemon juice, which "cook" the fish by denaturing the proteins. The fish is then mixed with other ingredients, such as onions, tomatoes, cilantro, and chili peppers. Ceviche is often served as an appetizer or as a light meal, and it is typically accompanied by tortilla chips or crackers. It is a healthy and flavorful dish that is low in calories and high in protein, vitamins, and minerals.

prep time

20 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

4-6 servings

Ingredients

1 pound of fresh white fish fillets, such as tilapia or snapper, diced into small pieces

1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lime juice

1/2 cup of freshly squeezed lemon juice

1/2 red onion, diced

1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and finely diced

1/4 cup of chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

Step 1

In a glass or ceramic bowl, combine the diced fish, lime juice, and lemon juice. Make sure the fish is fully covered with the citrus juice.

Step 2

Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or until the fish is opaque and has been "cooked" by the citrus juice.

Step 3

Once the fish is cooked, drain off any excess liquid and add the diced red onion, jalapeño pepper, and chopped cilantro to the bowl. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 4

Mix well and refrigerate for at least 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

Step 5

Serve the ceviche chilled with tortilla chips or crackers.
