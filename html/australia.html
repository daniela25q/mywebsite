Pavlova Dessert

Pavlova is a popular dessert in Australia and New Zealand, and there is some debate over which country actually invented it. However, it is generally agreed that the dessert was named after the Russian ballerina, Anna Pavlova, who toured both countries in the 1920s.

prep time

15-20 min.

cook time

60 min.

serving

8-10 servings

Ingredients

4 large egg whites, at room temperature

1 cup caster sugar

1 teaspoon white vinegar

1 teaspoon cornflour

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

1 cup thickened cream

Fresh fruit, such as strawberries, kiwi fruit, and passionfruit

Instructions

step 1

Preheat the oven to 150°C (300°F) and line a baking tray with baking paper.

step 2

Beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.

step 3

Gradually add the caster sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, while continuing to beat the egg whites until glossy and stiff.

step 4

Beat the egg whites with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form.

step 5

Spoon the mixture onto the prepared baking tray in a circular shape, using the back of a spoon to create a shallow well in the center.

step 6

Bake the pavlova in the preheated oven for 1 hour, or until the outside is crisp and dry but the center is still soft and marshmallow-like.

step 7

Turn off the oven and leave the pavlova inside to cool completely.

step 8

Once the pavlova has cooled, whip the thickened cream until it forms soft peaks.

step 9

Spoon the whipped cream into the well of the pavlova and top with fresh fruit.

step 10

Serve the pavlova immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.
