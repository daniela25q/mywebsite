Paella

Paella is a traditional Spanish dish that originated in the Valencia region of Spain. It is a rice dish that is typically made with saffron, chicken, rabbit, and seafood, but there are many variations of paella depending on the region and personal preference.

prep time

25 min.

cook time

35 min.

serving

4-6 servings

Ingredients

2 tablespoons olive oil

1 onion, chopped

3 cloves garlic, minced

2 cups Arborio or other short-grain rice

1 teaspoon saffron threads, crushed

1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika

4 cups chicken broth

1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined

1 pound mussels, scrubbed and debearded

1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed

1 red bell pepper, chopped

Salt and freshly ground black pepper

1 red bell pepper, chopped

Instructions

step 1

Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan or skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and red bell pepper and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes.

step 2

Add the garlic and rice and stir to coat with the oil. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently.

step 3

Stir in the saffron, smoked paprika, and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the liquid is mostly absorbed and the rice is tender but still slightly firm.

step 4

Arrange the shrimp and mussels over the top of the rice, then scatter the peas over the top. Cover the pan with a lid or foil and simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the shrimp are pink and cooked through and the mussels have opened.

step 5

Discard any unopened mussels and season the paella with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.

step 6

Serve the paella hot, garnished with lemon wedges.
